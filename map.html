District Profiles

The 10 administrative districts (quarters) of Saint Lucia

Castries

Capital City • Northwestern Coast

The capital and largest city, Castries is the commercial, administrative, and shipping hub. Home to the main port, government offices, the financial district, and major retail areas. Population ~22,000 (metro ~65,000).

  • Key Areas: Downtown, Pointe Seraphine (duty-free), La Place Carenage, Morne Fortune
  • Business: Banks, law firms, government HQ, Castries Market
  • Port: Cruise terminal, cargo port

Gros Islet

Tourism Hub • Northern Tip

The main tourism district, home to Rodney Bay (the island's tourism and entertainment center), luxury resorts, the national stadium, and the northern beaches.

  • Key Areas: Rodney Bay Village, Reduit Beach, Pigeon Island National Park, Cap Estate
  • Business: Hotels, restaurants, nightlife, marina, shopping mall
  • Known For: Friday Night Street Party (Gros Islet Jump-Up)

Vieux Fort

Second City • Southern Tip

The southernmost town and second-largest settlement. Home to the main international airport, free zone, and growing commercial area.

  • Key Areas: Hewanorra Int'l Airport, Vieux Fort Industrial Estate/Free Zone
  • Business: Light manufacturing, free zone operations, cargo port
  • Notable: Sandy Beach, Moule-a-Chique peninsula (southernmost point)

Soufriere

Heritage Town • Southwest Coast

The original French capital and home to St. Lucia's most iconic landmarks. A major eco-tourism destination with volcanic features.

  • Key Areas: The Pitons (UNESCO), Sulphur Springs, Diamond Falls, Fond Doux Estate
  • Business: Eco-tourism, luxury resorts (Jade Mountain, Sugar Beach), cocoa estates
  • Known For: World's only drive-in volcano

Dennery

Eastern District • Atlantic Coast

Located on the windward (Atlantic) coast. A largely agricultural and fishing community with authentic rural character.

  • Key Areas: Dennery Village, Fond d'Or Nature Reserve
  • Business: Fishing, agriculture (bananas, root crops), small-scale manufacturing
  • Known For: Dennery Fish Fiesta

Micoud

Eastern District • Southeast Coast

A large agricultural district on the eastern coast, important for banana and crop production.

  • Key Areas: Micoud Village, Praslin, Mamiku Gardens
  • Business: Agriculture (bananas, coconut, cocoa), fishing
  • Known For: La Rose & La Marguerite cultural festivals

Choiseul

Arts District • Southwest Coast

A southern district known for its arts and crafts heritage, pottery tradition, and agricultural communities.

  • Key Areas: Choiseul Village, Arts & Craft Centre, Balembouche Estate
  • Business: Pottery, handicrafts, agriculture, heritage tourism
  • Known For: Traditional Carib pottery and craft village

Laborie

Fishing Village • South Coast

A small, picturesque fishing village on the southern coast with strong community identity and authentic Creole culture.

  • Key Areas: Laborie Village, Laborie Bay
  • Business: Fishing, small-scale agriculture, community tourism
  • Known For: Traditional fishing, charming waterfront

Anse la Raye

Fishing Town • West Coast

A west coast fishing town famous for its weekly Friday Fish Fry and authentic local dining experience.

  • Key Areas: Anse la Raye Village, Anse Cochon Beach
  • Business: Fishing, food tourism, water sports
  • Known For: Friday Night Seafood Street Party

Canaries

Smallest District • West Coast

The smallest district, located between Anse la Raye and Soufriere. A quiet, rural community with stunning coastal scenery.

  • Key Areas: Canaries Village, Anse La Libert√© Beach
  • Business: Small-scale fishing, agriculture
  • Known For: Secluded beaches, peaceful setting
