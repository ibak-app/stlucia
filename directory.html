Banks & Financial Institutions

Banking Overview

Saint Lucia has 5 commercial banks supervised by the ECCB, plus international banks, a development bank, and 16 credit unions. The EC Dollar is pegged at XCD 2.70 = USD 1.00 since 1976. No exchange controls — funds can be freely converted and repatriated. The Bankers Association of St. Lucia (BASL) represents 10 member institutions.

Bank of Saint Lucia (BOSL)

Local Bank — Largest

The largest locally incorporated commercial bank, formed in 2001 from the merger of St. Lucia Co-operative Bank and National Commercial Bank. Full retail and corporate banking, investment banking division, mobile banking, DCash integration, and extensive POS network including wireless "Swipe on the Go" terminals.

Branches: 5 (Bridge St Castries, Waterfront Castries, Gros Islet, Vieux Fort, Soufriere) + Hewanorra Airport bureau de change

ATMs: 30 island-wide (branches, Massy Stores, malls, casino)

Website: bankofsaintlucia.com

1st National Bank St. Lucia Limited

Local Bank — Est. 1938

Oldest national bank (founded 1938 as St. Lucia Cooperative Bank). Acquired RBC's Saint Lucia operations in April 2021. Twice named "Bank of the Year" by the Financial Times. Operates a dedicated MSME Competency Unit — the only bank with a specialised SME unit. First bank to introduce mobile banking in Saint Lucia.

Website: 1stnationalbankonline.com

Eastern Caribbean Central Bank (ECCB)

Central Bank — Regional

Monetary authority for 8 ECCU states. Manages the XCD currency (pegged to USD at 2.70:1 since 1976). Supervises commercial banks. Operates the Regional Government Securities Market. DCash 2.0 (retail CBDC) anticipated 2026. Foreign reserves: ~EC$5.4B (98%+ backing ratio; statutory minimum 60%).

Website: eccb-centralbank.org

Saint Lucia Development Bank (SLDB)

Development Finance

Government-owned statutory corporation (est. 2009). Financing for agriculture, fisheries, housing, education, tourism, industry, SME development, and renewable energy. Accredited Green Climate Fund entity.

Website: sldb.lc

Financial Services Regulatory Authority (FSRA)

Regulator

Independent statutory body (est. 2014) regulating non-bank financial institutions: insurance companies, credit unions, money services businesses, pension funds, international banks, IBCs, mutual funds, and registered agents. 5 classes of money services licenses (remittance, currency exchange, cheque cashing, payment instruments, micro-lending).

Website: fsrastlucia.org
